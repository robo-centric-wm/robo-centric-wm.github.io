Pre-Training Robo-Centric World Models For Efficient Visual Control

Paper Code

Robo-Centric World Model

Abstract

Humans can accurately anticipate their movements to behave as expected during manipulation tasks. We are inspired to propose that integrating prior knowledge of robot dynamics into world models can effectively improve the sample efficiency of model-based reinforcement learning (MBRL) in visual robot control tasks. In this paper, we introduce the Robo-Centric World Model (RCWM), which is designed to decouple the dynamics of the robot from the environment and learn their interaction through a cross-attention mechanism. Specifically, we leverage prior robot masks in the pre-training phase to decouple robot dynamics and apply this knowledge to downstream tasks. Our approach enables the independent inference of robot dynamics from the environment, allowing for accurate dynamics predictions across a variety of tasks. In addition, we explore the advantages of the pre-trained RCWM by combining imitation learning and model-based reinforcement learning with a few expert trajectories, allowing us to learn an adequate policy and further accelerate subsequent online fine-tuning. Our results in Meta-world show that RCWM can effectively extract robot dynamics and significantly improve sample efficiency for downstream tasks compared to the vanilla world model in DreamerV3.

Method

we introduce the Robo-Centric World Model (RCWM), which can decouple the dynamics between the robot and the environment, and learn their interaction via an interaction model based on the cross-attention mechanism. With RCWM, we can extract robot dynamics through pre-training on upstream tasks with the assistance of prior robot masks, and use this prior knowledge about robot to improve sample efficiency on downstream tasks. Furthermore, we proposed few-shot expert-guided policy learning, which combines model-based reinforcement learning with behavioral cloning. This approach aims to learn an adequate policy from just a few expert trajectories to further accelerate subsequent online fine-tuning.

Results

Sample Efficiency Improvement

Compared to the vanilla world model in Dreamerv3, the pre-trained RCWM can utilize the extracted prior knowledge of robot dynamics to further improve the sample efficiency of downstream tasks.

Few-shot Expert-guided Policy Learning

With extracted robot dynamics, the pre-trained RCWM can provide effective representations and predictions by fine-tuning on just 50 expert trajectories. The actor can be trained by combining behavioral cloning with model-based reinforcement learning to learn an adequate policy that can further accelerate subsequent online fine-tuning.

Robot Dynamics Extraction

The pre-trained RCWM can consistently deliver accurate predictions of robot movements in response to action signals when applied on unseen downstream tasks. Even when environmental observations are replaced with random noise, the robot branch can make accurate predictions almost unaffectedly.

Robot-Object Interaction

The interaction model in RCWM can effectively utilize the cross-attention mechanism to capture how the predicted movement of the robot will impact the state of the environment. The environment branch can accurately generate predictions that align with the robot movement predicted by the robot branch.
