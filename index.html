Pre-Training Robo-Centric World Models For Efficient Visual Control

Paper Code

Abstract

Model-based reinforcement learning has the potential to efficiently solve visual robot control tasks but remains limited by model errors. Current approaches desire to further improve the sample efficiency by pretraining world models. However, these methods typically focus on learning efficient visual representations rather than accurate dynamic predictions. In this paper, we introduce Robo-Centric World Models (RCWM), which decouple the dynamics of the robot and the environment separately, and preserve the robot dynamics branch during downstream task fine-tuning to improve robot dynamics prediction accuracy. Specifically, we utilize the structured prior robot mask in the pre-training phase to guide the decoupling of the dynamics and capture the effect of the robot's motion on the environment through the attention mechanism. Moreover, we further explore the advantages of RCWM in the few-shot setting. With RCWM, we can use a few demonstrations to obtain a robust initial policy without interacting with the environment. Our results in the Meta-world environment demonstrate that pre-trained RCWM can effectively capture robot dynamics and improve sample efficiency for downstream tasks.
